CITY OF LANDMARKS.

Mazar-e-Quaid

The pure white marble mausoleum is the final resting place of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founder of Pakistan. Completed in 1970, it is a masterpiece of modern Islamic architecture and a symbol of national pride.

Mohatta Palace

Built in 1927 as a summer home for a businessman from Rajasthan, this palace is a stunning blend of Rajput and Mughal styles. Today, it serves as a premier museum hosting international art and history exhibitions.

Frere Hall

A relic of the British Raj, Frere Hall is a Venetian-Gothic masterpiece. It houses a vast library and a world-famous ceiling mural titled 'Arz-o-Samawat' painted by the legendary artist Sadequain.

Clifton Beach

The city's most popular coastline, Clifton Beach (Sea View) is the soul of Karachi's leisure life. From camel rides and beach buggies to the famous street food stalls, it is where the city comes to breathe.
